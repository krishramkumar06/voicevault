VoiceVault 1.0.0 — the first release.

Turn your Apple Voice Memos into a searchable, linked Obsidian vault. Entirely on your Mac: Apple's on-device transcription, a local summarizer via Ollama, no cloud, no account, no terminal.

**What's in the box**
- Reads your real Voice Memos library with real titles (or any folder of audio files)
- One Markdown note per memo: verbatim transcript, short summary, key points, suggested tags, `[[people]]` links
- Review every note before it's written — nothing touches your vault without your say-so
- Name correction: teach it your people (Suren, not "Soren") and it fixes mishearings phonetically, logging every change
- The summary model and prompt are fully editable in Settings
- Guided setup: the app installs and manages the local AI engine for you

**Requirements:** macOS 26 (Tahoe), Apple Silicon.

**Installing:** unzip, drag VoiceVault to Applications, then **right-click → Open** the first time (the app isn't notarized yet — see the README for the one-time steps).
